Djordje Balasevic

Singer and songwriter, writer, poet and director

Balasevic was a prolific singer and writer, he recorded 14 albums and wrote 8 books during his career and has been characterised by media and commentators as "musical legend" and author of many "evergreen hits".

Here's a time line of Balasevic's life and career:

  • 1953 - Born in Novi Sad, Yugoslavia
  • 1977 - Joines the group Zetva and records a humorous tango-oriented hit single "U razdeljak te ljubim" (I Lay a Kiss on Your Parting), which is sold in more than 180,000 copies.
  • 1978 - Leaves Zetva ,and together with Verica Todorovic, forms the band Rani Mraz. The band has its debut at the music festival in Opatija with the song "Moja prva ljubav" (My First Love).
  • 1979 - Records Rani Mraz's first album Mojoj mami umesto maturske slike u izlogu (To my Mom instead of Prom Photo in the Shop-Window). Same year, at the Split Festival, wins the first prize with the single "Panonski mornar" (Pannonian Sailor).
  • 1980 - Releases second and final album under the name Rani Mraz, with a symbolic title Odlazi cirkus (The Circus Is Leaving). The album deliveres several hit songs, one of them is "Priča o Vasi Ladackom" (Story of Vasa Ladacki) which went on to become one of Balasevic's signature songs.
  • 1980 - First child, daughter Jovana is born.
  • 1981 - He marries Olivera on May 7 in Novi Sad.
  • 1982 - Starts his solo career with the album Pub (Jack) which brings hits "Boza zvani Pub" (Boza Known as the Jack), "Pesma o jednom petlu" (The Song about a Rooster), "Lepa protina kci" (Archpriest's Beautiful Daughter) and "Ratnik paorskog srca" (Warrior with Peasant's Heart).
  • 1983 - Releases the album Celovecernji the Kid (Wholevening the Kid), which features hits "Svirajte mi 'Jesen stize, dunjo moja'" (Play 'Autumn Is Coming, My Dear' to Me), "Neko to od gore vidi sve" (Someone from up above Watches it All), "Blues mutne vode" (Muddy Water Blues), "Lunjo" (Hey, Tramp) and "Don Francisco Long Play".
  • 1984 - Second child, daughter Jelena is born
  • 1985 - Releases the following album, 003, and brings hits "Slovenska" (The Slavic Song), "Al' se nekad dobro jelo" (Back Then Eating Was Good), "Badnje vece" (Christmas Eve) and "Olivera".
  • 1986 - Releases the album Bezdan (Abyss), which brings hits "Ne lomite mi bagrenje" (Don't Break My Locust Trees), "Bezdan" and "Ne volim januar" (I Don't Like January).
  • 1987 - Releases his first live album, double album U tvojim molitvama - Balade (In Your Prayers - Ballads). The album was recorded during 1986 and 1987 on his concerts in Zetra hall in Sarajevo, Ledena dvorana and Salata in Zagreb, Sava Centar in Belgrade, and Studio M in Novi Sad.
  • 1988 - Releases the following album Panta Rei. The song "Requiem" was dedicated to late Josip Broz Tito, while satire "Soliter" (High-rise) caricatures Yugoslavia as a building in which only façade still holds while foundations slide.
  • 1989 - Releases Tri posleratna druga (Three postwar Friends). The album features radio hits "Kad odem" (When I'm Gone), "D-moll" (D minor), "Caletova pesma" (Dad's Song), "Saputnik" (Fellow Traveler), "O, Boze" (Oh God), and folk-oriented "Devojka sa cardas nogama" (A Girl with Csárdás Legs).
  • 1991 - Releases the album Marim ja... (I care...). The songs "Nevernik" (The Unbeliever), "Ringispil" (Carousel), "Divlji badem" (Wild Almond) are the album's biggest hits.
  • 1993 - Releases his next album Jedan od onih zivota... (One of Those Lives...). The songs such as "Krivi smo mi" (It's Our Fault) and "Covek sa mesecom u ocima" (The Man with the Moon in His Eyes) heavily criticize and denounce the ongoing war. At the same time, releases the compilation album Najveci hitovi (Greatest hits), featuring songs recorded during the 1986-1991 period.
  • 1994 - Third child, son Aleksa is born
  • 1996 - Releases Naposletku... (After All...). Na posletku... is mainly folk rock-oriented. Nearly all instruments on the album are acoustic, with the violin becoming dominant and woodwind instruments heavily used.
  • 1997 - Releases the live album Da l' je sve bilo samo fol?, recorded on 6 December 1996 on a concert in Maribor, Slovenia.
  • 2000 - Releases his most politically involved album, Devedesete (Nineties).
  • 2001 - Releases the album Dnevnik starog momka (Diary of an Old Bachelor). The album comprises 12 songs, each having a female name as its title. The song titles form the acrostic "Olja je najbolja" (Olja is the Best). Olja is the nickname of Balasevic's wife Olivera Balasevic.
  • 2002 - Releases the compilation album Ostace okrugli trag na mestu satre (A Round Trace Will Remain at the Place where Tent Used to Be), named after a verse from the song "Odlazi cirkus".
  • 2010 - Directing the film Kao rani mraz (Like An Early Frost).
  • 2012 - Releases six new songs among which are: "Berba '59" (Harvest of '59), "Ljubav ne pobedjuje" (Love Doesn't Win) and "Osmeh se vratio u grad" (Smile Returned to Town).
  • 2015 - Releases a song "Duet" dedicated to Kemal Monteno who had died in January of that year.[71] In 2016, Balašević released a song "Mala vidra sa Begeja" (Little otter from Bega river).[72]
  • 2016 - Releases a song "Mala vidra sa Begeja" (Little otter from Bega river).
  • 2019 - Sufferes a myocardial infarction from which he was recovering until his death.
  • 2021 - Dies on 19 February, in Novi Sad from complications of pneumonia.

    Bibliography

  • Racunajte na nas - book of poems
  • I zivot ide dalje - collection of columns
  • Jedan od onih zivota - novel
  • Dodir svile (1998) - book of poems
  • Tri posleratna druga - novel
  • ...i od dva-tri akorda (jer ni ne umem bolje ja...) - book of poems
  • Kao rani mraz - screenplay based on which a movie with the same name was filmed
  • Kalendar mog detinjstva - novel

News of his death was widely shared across Croatian and Bosnian media, describing Balasevic as a "legendary" performer whose songs could "inspire deepest emotion in an audience." In his hometown of Novi Sad, hundreds of people gathered in the town center after the news of his death, and besides in his native Serbia there were fan gatherings as well as in Zagreb and Pula in Croatia and in Sarajevo in Bosnia and Herzegovina. The Mayor of Novi Sad, Milos Vucevic announced that on February 21, day of mourning will be held in Novi Sad because of Balsevic's death. On 22 February 2021, an event called Noc kada je ĐDjole preplivao Dunav (eng. Night when Djole crossed the Danube) was organized in Novi Sad in memory of him. One of the participants was the Serbian National Theater choir.
